# Goals

- Document examples of large systems that succeed through resource-conscious design.
- Explain how natural laws encourage efficient behavior in cosmic structures.
- Offer practical insights for developers building with the Unit system.
# Introduction

In an age defined by abundance and excess, it can be difficult to look beyond the prominent features of the world's great entities—our governments, our corporations, and the celestial bodies in the sky. Yet these "greater bodies" offer subtle lessons in restraint and economy. This compendium seeks to granulate those lessons: carefully breaking down the patterns, behaviors, and internal mechanisms that allow large systems to conserve resources, streamline operations, and flourish by embracing the art of "less."

We will examine institutions that, despite their vast influence, maintain minimal and efficient structures. We will also look at natural formations whose evolution demonstrates how the laws of physics encourage frugality on a cosmic scale. By gathering accounts and reflections from multiple disciplines, this compendium reveals frugality as a unifying principle that benefits organizations and individuals alike.

Alongside these studies, we introduce planar concepts that wormhole through the system as a self-contained unit. These ideas cross layers of abstraction, linking disparate disciplines to show how minimal constructs can open direct paths to greater complexity.
